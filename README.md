Sacha Baron Cohen — Corporate & Authorship Profile
The Film principal, argued from production method rather than from filings
One of the atomized principal profiles in the Banksy-as-corporate-person investigation. Unlike Hack, whose case rests on a documented corporate paper trail, Cohen's case rests mainly on production-method and authorship analysis — which is correct, because he is a performer-author, not a company-builder, and his documented corporate footprint, while real, contains no filed link to the Banksy set. The hypothesis that he is the Film division's principal — Exit's authorial showrunner and the spoken/on-camera Banksy persona — is mine, held at high confidence, and built from the kinds of evidence noted below, each weighted for what it is. Cohen has been publicly named in this hypothesis since 2022 and has been free to correct it.

What the corporate record shows
Cohen is a documented production-company principal — which establishes capacity, not connection. His vehicle history is consistent and long-running, almost all of it registered at 5 Elstree Gate, Borehamwood:

Mountport Limited (04059031) and Ali G Limited (04066496) — both 2000, the earliest vehicles, from the Ali G period.
Four By Two Limited (04427884) — incorporated 30 April 2002, his principal production/loan-out company. (The name is his own joke: "four by two" is Cockney rhyming slang.) Long-dormant in recent filings but continuously maintained.
Twinkletoes Productions (09067212, 2014), and a later cluster — Wildwood, Heath, Holne Chase, Pondstead, Tumulus Productions (2022–2024) — single-purpose production vehicles of the kind a working film principal generates per project or slate.
Baron Cohen Films Limited (07904094) — incorporated 2012, dissolved 2013, a short-lived vehicle.

A documented structural feature worth noting, because it echoes another principal in this investigation: Cohen's companies show a family-officer pattern. Four By Two carried his father Gerald Baron Cohen as secretary/director, and Baron Cohen Films carried Hyman Vivian Baron Cohen and his brother Ashley Louis Baron Cohen. This is the same embedded-family-administration structure seen at Hack's Dazed group (Susanne Waddell, Rankin's sister) — family-held creative businesses, run close. That is an observation about structure, not a link between the two.
The decisive negative, stated plainly: no Cohen company links by any filing — shared officer, shared address, shared parent — to Paranoid Pictures or any Banksy vehicle. His corporate world is entirely his own (Borehamwood, his family, his projects). So the corporate record establishes that he is a genuine production principal of the right kind, and supplies no documentary connection to Banksy. The connection, if it exists, is not corporate.
The authorship argument — the real spine
The strong case is not a filing; it is authorship. Cohen invented and ran a specific film genre: the low-budget, reality-scripted, identity-prank feature, incubated on British sketch television in the mid-1990s and built into Ali G, Borat, and Brüno. In those films he is not a hired actor — he is the author-principal who originates the property, sets the method, and is the reason the production exists.
The operative structural fact is this: in his own films, Cohen cannot run the floor during physical production, because he is in character the entire shoot. The credited director therefore functions as the executant — the showrunner beneath the principal — while the principal performs. That is a documented feature of how his films are made.
That structure maps onto Exit Through the Gift Shop with unusual precision. Exit is a low-budget, reality-scripted feature mixing a reality-scripted central figure (Thierry Guetta / "Mr. Brainwash") with real artists — squarely the genre Cohen originated — with a credited director (Jaimie D'Cruz) running the floor. The Costner-on-Dances with Wolves / Gibson-on-Braveheart principle applies: a performer who originates and drives a production is its authorial principal even when others hold the camera and even when he is also performing in it. On the authorship-and-method evidence, reading Exit as sitting inside Cohen's genre, made by his method, is well-founded.
What "director" means in reality-scripted production
The director credit in this genre carries less than it does in conventional film, and that distinction matters for reading Exit. In the Borat and Brüno model, the credited director directs the principal through staged confrontations with unwitting real people — but the "scenes" are real events captured run-and-gun, not sequences a director blocks, lights, and calls in the ordinary way. A large part of the directorial contribution lands in post-production: assembling reality-captured footage into a narrative, cutting it, scoring it, shaping the voiceover, approving the final edit. The on-set authorship belongs to the principal who designs and performs the situations.
Applied to Exit, this is the structural reason the director credit and the authorial principal can come apart. Exit's raw material is reality-captured — street artists filmed at work, Guetta's years of footage, the Mr. Brainwash rise — none of it requiring conventional scene-direction. The single element needing genuine on-set handling is the obscured, voice-altered Banksy interview, which is minimal. The credited director's decisive work is therefore concentrated in post: the voiceover, the music, the final cut. That makes the Banksy/D'Cruz directing credit, in my reading, both legitimate and largely nominal — legitimate because someone did assemble the film, nominal because the genre puts the authorship in the design of the situations and the performance, not in the floor-direction.
(Where this tips into speculation, and is held as such: the specific division of labour — that the on-shoot presence with Laz and "Banksy" was minimal, that even the Banksy interview could have been captured by Lazarides rather than the credited director, that Cohen's persona voice was laid in during post — is my inference about who did what, not a documented allocation. The structural point about the genre is defensible; the task-by-task attribution is hypothesis. One supporting recollection, flagged as exactly that: I recall Stephen Lazarides discussing the shoot and confirming its modest scale in a television interview, which I have not been able to relocate. I record it as a recollection I cannot currently source — not as a citation — because an interview I can't produce is not evidence, and it is weighed accordingly. If located, it would be on-record corroboration from a named principal that the production was small, which is the load-bearing fact under the minimal-on-set-direction reading.)
The documented Exit production frame
What the credits document: Exit is "A Paranoid Pictures film," produced by Jaimie D'Cruz, executive producers Holly Cushing and James Gay-Rees, international sales Cinetic Media (New York), US distribution Oscilloscope and Producers Distribution Agency, listed as a UK/US co-production. Banksy is credited as director; his face is obscured and voice altered.
The two-picture structure is documented and notable: Exit (2010 feature) and The Antics Roadshow (2011, a 48-minute Channel 4 piece, also a Paranoid Pictures production, also with D'Cruz) aired in a paired broadcast — Antics immediately following Exit's UK television premiere on 13 August 2011. A feature plus a cheap throwaway TV piece, same producer vehicle, same director, broadcast together: a structure consistent with a bundled two-picture deal. The interpretation that Antics functioned as a rights-retention or contract-completion production — in the manner of the well-documented Corman/Eichinger Fantastic Four case — is mine, an interpretive frame for why an otherwise purposeless throwaway exists. The paired broadcast and shared production team are documented; the deal-structure reading is inference.
Public linkages
Cohen's documented public ties to the Banksy world are circumstantial but real, and belong in the record as such — colour, not proof: his appearance at Banksy's Crude Oils show; a reported exchange of a Banksy work around the Exit period; and his on-record remark expressing that he wished he were Banksy. None of these establishes the role; together they place him repeatedly and publicly adjacent to the project at the relevant moments.
The informant
A source who began working for Shepard Fairey told me, directly, that Cohen show-ran the Fairey/Guetta footage — the around-the-world material shot on consecutive weekends with rotating crews prepping and wrapping locations, with the two reality-scripted principals and the real artists filmed within it. I judge the source credible and the detail specific.
It must be weighed for exactly what it is: a single, unnamed, second-hand source, uncorroborated by any document or public credit, relayed through me having relayed through the source. It is not proof, and the credited showrunner-executant on the record is D'Cruz. I record the informant's account because it is part of the investigative trail and because it is consistent with the authorship argument above — but it is testimony, held as testimony, not established fact. If it is ever corroborated, it moves up; until then it sits here, attributed and flagged.
The hypothesis
Drawing these together: Cohen is the Film-division principal — the authorial showrunner above Exit's credited director and the spoken/on-camera Banksy persona. This is my high-confidence hypothesis. Its strength is the authorship-and-method argument, which is genuinely substantial; its support includes the documented production frame, the public linkages, and the informant's account; its limit is that no filing connects his companies to the Banksy set and no credit names him in the role. The genre is documentably his. That he applied it to Banksy is the inference — well-founded, and still an inference.

Method note: Cohen's production-company footprint and the Exit production credits are documented. The authorship argument — Exit as a film in the genre he created, made by his principal-in-character / director-beneath method — is analysis built on his documented filmography and Exit's documented form. The public linkages are real and circumstantial. The informant's showrunner account is attributed testimony, uncorroborated. The persona/showrunner conclusion is hypothesis. No filed link between any Cohen company and the Banksy set exists. The discipline is the deliverable.
